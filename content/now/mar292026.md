# optimism

back when i was in middle school and also a big part of high school, i always saw myself as a glass-half-empty kind of person. it's not hard to understand why, given that my parents have always been tough, classic immigrant mentality basically. it doesn't help that i didn't match the mold of an immigrant kid with helicopter parents. maybe one day i'll talk about how i threw a project x-esque 16th birthday party and the cops came in to kick people out and someone carved a hole in my bathroom wall with a knife... anyhow OK.

MY POINT IS im realizing now that i've probably always been an optimist wrapped in a teenage-depression outer layer. and as i got into uni (which my parents were so stressed i was never going to go, bc that's ... also what I thought HAHA) and obviously after graduation i think i neutralized in some way.

but it wasn't until i got into startups that the optimism really shown through. and it's because you need the optimism.

to bring something into the world that DOESN'T EXIST AT ALL is sort of wild if you think about it.

you could never convince me to work on a gambling app or anything liek that though. don't get me wrong, it's HARD SHIT to do no matter what you're doing, but i sort of want to be on the 'good' side of history.

what does good mean though?

i think because of my upbringing and environment, i just .... feel very strongly "for the people".

(also if its not clear by now i write these notes for myself inb4 youre like OMG UR SO PERFORMATIVE LOL, this is literally my inner dialogue and im not sure if its clear but its not like im editing this shit...)

but yes anyhow "for the people" -- bc so many of my closer hs friends never actually went to college, and i had a job every since i was 15 (and not some cool techy tutoring job lol). i just get how it feels to be a regular person.

to work a min wage job, to feel like the only thing you can do is the thing ahead of you. to NOT feel like anything and everything is possible. because while it is... it's so freaking hard. i get that mindset because thats genuinely the mindset i grew up with.

with this whole transition into an AI world, i just know first hand how it feels to be part of this "permanent underclass". to feel like all the work you've done for the past 30 years of growing and refining your craft is all of a sudden being taken away. what it really means when "AI IS INCREASING EFFICIENCY" and the human impacts (financial and emotional and social) of that.

stuff to think about right?
